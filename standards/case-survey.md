# Case Survey Standard
Source: ACM SIGSOFT Empirical Standards, CC0.

## Essential Attributes
- Presents a step-by-step, replicable description of the search process
  for published case studies
- Defines clear inclusion and exclusion criteria
- Describes the sampling strategy
- Mitigates sampling and publication bias
- Defines a coding scheme to convert qualitative case descriptions
  into quantitative variables
- Either describes the coding scheme in detail or provides it as supplementary material
- Clearly explains how missingness in the dataset was managed
- Draws conclusions based on the quantitative variables derived
- Acknowledges generalizability threats; discusses how reviewed cases may differ
  from the target population

## Desirable Attributes
- Provides supplementary materials
  (protocol, search terms, selection results, coding scheme, decision rules, dataset)
- Explains and justifies the design of the coding scheme
- Uses 2+ independent analysts; reports inter-rater reliability;
  explains how discrepancies were resolved
- Assesses quality of primary studies; models study quality as a moderating variable
- Consolidates results using tables, diagrams, or charts including PRISMA flow diagram

## Invalid Criticisms
- The studies were not published in peer-reviewed venues
- The original studies do not employ a common research design
- The cases are not a random sample of the phenomenon
