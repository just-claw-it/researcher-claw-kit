# Experiment (with Human Participants) Standard
Source: ACM SIGSOFT Empirical Standards, CC0.

## Essential Attributes
- States formal hypotheses
- Uses two-sided hypotheses OR justifies one-sided hypotheses
- Describes dependent variable(s) and how they are measured (units, instruments)
- Describes independent variable(s) and how they are manipulated or measured
- Describes extraneous variables and how they are controlled
- Justifies sample size (e.g. via power analysis)
- Describes how phenomenon characteristics relate to experimental constructs
- Describes research design and protocol (treatments, tasks, design, allocation, logistics)
- EITHER uses random assignment with logistics explained
  OR justifies non-random assignment and explains how unequal groups threat is mitigated
- Describes experimental objects and their characteristics
- Justifies selection of experimental objects; acknowledges object-treatment confounds
- Confirms no incentives offered OR describes incentives used
- Describes participants (age, gender, education, relevant experience)
- Reports distribution-appropriate descriptive and inferential statistics; justifies tests
- Reports effect sizes with confidence intervals (frequentist approach)
- Discusses construct, conclusion, internal, and external validity
- Discusses alternative interpretations of results

## Desirable Attributes
- Provides supplementary materials (protocol, tasks, raw de-identified data, analysis scripts)
- Justifies hypotheses based on previous studies and theory
- Discusses alternative experimental designs and validity trade-offs
- Includes visualizations of data distributions
- Named experiment design (e.g. 2x2 factorial, randomized block)
- Reports manipulation checks
- Pre-registration of hypotheses and design
- Clearly distinguishes evidence-based results from speculation

## General Quality Criteria
Conclusion validity, construct validity, internal validity, reliability, objectivity, reproducibility.

## Invalid Criticisms
- Participants are students (appropriateness depends on context and technology under evaluation)
- Low external validity
- The experiment is a replication
- Not enough participants unless supported by power analysis
- Not employing an incentivization scheme
