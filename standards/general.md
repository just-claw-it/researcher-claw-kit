# General Standard
Applies to ALL empirical papers regardless of method.
Source: ACM SIGSOFT Empirical Standards, CC0.

## Essential Attributes
- States a purpose, problem, objective, or research question
- Explains why the purpose is important (motivation)
- Defines jargon, acronyms, and key concepts
- Names the methodology used
- Methodology is appropriate for the stated purpose
- Describes in detail what, where, when, and how data were collected
- Describes in detail how data were analyzed
- Presents results
- Results directly address research questions
- Enumerates and validates assumptions of statistical tests used (if any)
- Discusses implications of results
- Discloses all major limitations
- States clear conclusions linked to research questions, supported by evidence
- Contributes to the collective body of knowledge
- Language is not misleading
- Visualizations are not misleading

## Desirable Attributes
- States epistemological stance (e.g. positivism, interpretivism, critical realism)
- Synthesizes related work (not just lists it)
- Clearly describes relationship between contribution and related work
- Demonstrates statistical power (quantitative) or saturation (qualitative)
- Discusses attempts to mitigate limitations
- Provides recommendations for practice, education, or research
- Clarifies roles and responsibilities of researchers
- Provides author self-reflection

## Invalid Criticisms
- Arbitrary minimum sample sizes without power analysis
- "Lacks detail" without specifying what detail
- "Not novel" without citing a practically identical prior contribution
- Rejecting a study for replicating existing work
