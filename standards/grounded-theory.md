# Grounded Theory Standard
Source: ACM SIGSOFT Empirical Standards, CC0.

## Essential Attributes
- Identifies the variant(s) of Grounded Theory used (Glaser, Strauss/Corbin, Charmaz, etc.)
- Characterizes the data (participant demographics, document types, nature of field notes)
- Explains how research iterated between data collection (theoretical sampling)
  and analysis (constant comparison)
- States that and how theoretical saturation was determined
- Explains how key patterns (categories) were found via GT steps (e.g. selective coding)
- States results in terms of concepts that assign meaning to data
  (does NOT merely describe the data)
- Grounds important concepts in raw data explicitly (e.g. via quotations)
- Discusses the transferability of the theory

## Desirable Attributes
- Provides supplementary materials (interview guides, coding schemes, coding examples)
- Results involve relationships between concepts (not just unconnected themes)
- A diagram provides an overview of main concepts and relationships
- Shows mapping from categories to lower-level codes and observations
- Explains how and why the study deviates from the claimed GT variant (if applicable)
- Includes data from a diverse spectrum of participants
- Explains how memo writing drove the work
- Validates results using member checking or research audits
- Compares results with prior theory or related research
- Reflects on how researcher biases may have affected analysis

## General Quality Criteria
Credibility, resonance, usefulness, and whether results extend cumulative knowledge.
Quantitative criteria (internal validity, reliability, generalizability) do NOT apply.

## Antipatterns to flag in the paper
- Conducting data collection and analysis sequentially, not iteratively
- Data analysis focuses on counting codes instead of interpreting them
- Small, homogeneous samples creating the illusion of theoretical saturation

## Invalid Criticisms
- Lack of quantitative data, causal analysis, objectivity, or generalizability
- Lack of replicability or transcript release
- Research questions should have been different
- Findings should have been presented differently
