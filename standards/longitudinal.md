# Longitudinal Study Standard
Source: ACM SIGSOFT Empirical Standards, CC0.

## Essential Attributes
- Determines the appropriate number of waves based on the natural oscillation
  of the research phenomenon
- Uses at least two data collection waves
- Subjects (humans or artifacts) are identifiable between waves
- Justifies the data analysis strategy
- Data analysis strategy is appropriate for the interdependent nature of longitudinal data
- Discusses the critical alpha levels or justifies Bayesian priors
- Justifies sample size (e.g. via power analysis)
- Describes data loss throughout the different waves
- Explains how missing data are handled
- Describes the subjects (e.g. demographic information for human subjects)
- Discusses the operationalization of the research model (construct validity)

## Desirable Attributes
- Provides supplementary materials (datasets, instruments, analytical scripts,
  reproduction instructions)
- Either builds new theory or tests existing theory
- Investigates causality using the longitudinal nature of data to establish precedence
- Discusses potential confounding factors that cannot be statistically controlled
- Discusses data consistency across waves (e.g. test-retest reliability)
- Examines differences in distributions between waves
- Addresses survivorship bias

## General Quality Criteria
Reliability, internal validity, conclusion validity, construct validity, external validity.
Longitudinal studies exploit temporal data to maximize internal validity;
other criteria are sometimes sacrificed for this.

## Invalid Criticisms
- The time span between measurements is too short or too long
- The number of waves is inadequate without a reasoned explanation
- The sample size is too small without a post hoc power calculation
- Complaining about generalizability when the paper clearly acknowledges the limitation
