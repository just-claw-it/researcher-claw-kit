# Meta-Science / Methodological Guidelines Standard
Source: ACM SIGSOFT Empirical Standards, CC0.

## Essential Attributes
- Presents information that is useful for other researchers
- Makes recommendations for future research
- Presents clear, valid arguments supporting those recommendations

## Desirable Attributes
- Synthesizes related work from reference disciplines
- Provides insight specifically for software engineering —
  goes beyond summarizing existing guidance
- Results integrated into prior theory or research
- Develops helpful artifacts (checklists, templates, tools, criteria sets)

## General Quality Criteria
- Comprehensiveness of analysis or guidance
- Usefulness to the research community
- Quality of argumentation supporting the analysis
- Degree of integration with previous work in SE and reference disciplines

## Important note for reviewers
Because meta-scientific claims often cannot be empirically tested,
reviewers must themselves be experts in the methodology to evaluate
the reasonableness of the guidelines. Standards are higher for
the discussion and guidelines sections than for an empirical paper.

## Invalid Criticisms
- Guidelines are not based on empirical evidence
  (empirically testing meta-scientific propositions is typically impossible;
  face validity, comprehensiveness, and usefulness are the right criteria)
