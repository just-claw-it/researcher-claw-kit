# Mixed Methods Standard
Source: ACM SIGSOFT Empirical Standards, CC0.
IMPORTANT: Also apply the standard for EACH individual method used.

## Essential Attributes
- Justifies using multiple methodologies
- Provides a purpose statement conveying the multi-method design intent (why)
- Describes the multi-method or mixed method design (what)
- Describes which phases of the research use which methods (when)
- Describes how the design aligns with the research question
- Integrates the findings from ALL methods to address the research question
- Acknowledges limitations of integrating findings across methods

## Desirable Attributes
- Defines the multi-method design used
- Describes and justifies sample reuse (or non-reuse) across methods
- Illustrates the research design using a visual model
- Discusses discrepancies and incongruent findings across methods
- Describes the philosophical/epistemological foundations underlying the design
- Describes challenges in the design and how they were or could be mitigated

## Antipatterns to flag in the paper
- Uninvited guest: a method appears in discussion but was not introduced in methodology
- Smoke and mirrors: one method contributes only tokenistically to the findings
- Integration failure: findings from the methods are never actually integrated
- Cargo cult: the team lacks expertise in one of the methods used
- Sample contamination: same participants used across sequential methods
  without accounting for contamination from earlier phases

## Invalid Criticisms
- Methods do not contribute equally (unequal designs are a legitimate choice)
- Methods have different philosophical foundations (acknowledged incompatibility can be managed)
- The wrong method is dominant in an unequal design (this is a design choice, not a flaw)
- Methods produce inconsistent findings (inconsistency is informative, not a flaw)
