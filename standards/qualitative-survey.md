# Qualitative Survey Standard
Source: ACM SIGSOFT Empirical Standards, CC0.

## Essential Attributes
- Defines the target population and the frame used to access it
- Describes the sampling strategy and justifies it
- Describes how data were collected (e.g. interview guide, observation protocol)
- Describes participant demographics and work roles
- Describes how data were analyzed (e.g. coding scheme, thematic analysis)
- Presents results as themes, categories, or patterns — not just raw quotes
- Results are grounded in data (quotations used as evidence, not as the only representation)
- Discusses transferability of findings to other contexts

## Desirable Attributes
- Provides supplementary materials (interview guide, coding scheme, sample quotes per theme)
- Uses 2+ coders with inter-rater reliability reported
- Validates results via member checking or practitioner feedback
- Distinguishes between frequency of mention and importance of finding
- Reflects on researcher biases and how they were managed
- Discusses saturation — when and why data collection stopped

## General Quality Criteria
Credibility, transferability, dependability, confirmability.
Quantitative criteria (validity, reliability, generalizability in a statistical sense)
typically do NOT apply.

## Invalid Criticisms
- Sample is not random or representative
- Sample size is too small without a saturation argument
- Findings cannot be generalized statistically
  (qualitative surveys aim for theoretical, not statistical, generalization)
