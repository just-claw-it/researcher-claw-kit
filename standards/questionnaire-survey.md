# Questionnaire Survey Standard
Source: ACM SIGSOFT Empirical Standards, CC0.

## Essential Attributes
- Defines the target population clearly
- Describes the sampling strategy and justifies it
- Reports response rate and discusses non-response bias
- Describes how the questionnaire was designed and validated
  (e.g. pilot testing, expert review)
- Describes the measurement instrument (scales, items, response options)
- Reports reliability of multi-item scales (e.g. Cronbach's alpha)
- Reports distribution-appropriate descriptive and inferential statistics
- Discusses construct validity (does the instrument measure what it claims to?)
- Discusses common method bias and how it was addressed
- Discloses all major limitations including sampling limitations

## Desirable Attributes
- Provides the questionnaire instrument as supplementary material
- Reports confirmatory or exploratory factor analysis for multi-item scales
- Pre-registers hypotheses
- Discusses effect sizes and practical significance, not just statistical significance
- Considers and reports dropout patterns
- Clearly distinguishes evidence-based results from speculation

## General Quality Criteria
Reliability, construct validity, internal validity, conclusion validity, external validity.

## Invalid Criticisms
- Low response rate alone (must also argue that non-response bias is a threat)
- Sample is not perfectly representative (discuss the limitation, do not reject for it)
