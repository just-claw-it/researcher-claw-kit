# Replication Standard
Source: ACM SIGSOFT Empirical Standards, CC0.

## Essential Attributes
- Clearly identifies the original study being replicated (full citation)
- States the replication type:
  exact (same protocol, same population) |
  conceptual (same hypotheses, different operationalization) |
  differentiated (systematic variation of one or more design elements)
- Justifies why this study is worth replicating
- Describes deviations from the original study and explains each deviation
- Compares results with the original study directly
- Discusses whether and why results agree or disagree with the original
- Does not treat inconsistency with the original as a flaw to hide

## Desirable Attributes
- Involves at least one researcher independent of the original study
- Pre-registers the replication protocol
- Provides a replication package
- Conducts a meta-analysis if multiple replications of the same study exist
- Discusses what the replication adds beyond confirming the original

## Invalid Criticisms
- The replication merely confirms the findings of the original study
  (confirmation is a legitimate scientific outcome)
- The study is not novel because it replicates prior work
  (replication is a first-class research contribution)
- The sample or context differs from the original
  (differentiated replications are intentional and valid)
